Alan Turing

The father of computer science

Timeline

  • 1912-Born in Maida Vale, London, England, United Kingdom.
  • 1922-1926-Attended Hazelhurst Preparatory School.
  • 1926-1930-Enrolled at Sherborne School. Became friends with fellow student Christopher Morcom, who would die in 1930, causing Turing tremendous grief.
  • 1931-1934-Completed undergraduate studies in mathematics at King's College, Cambridge.
  • 1936-Publishes "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem." Introduces the concept of the Turing machine.
  • 1936-1938-Studies under Alonzo Church at Princeton.
  • 1938-Obtains PhD from Princeton with the dissertation, Systems of Logic Based on Ordinals.
  • 1939-1940-Helps the Allies crack the Enigma code by using the Bombe.
  • 1942-Helps crack the German Naval Enigma system.
  • 1952-Publishes "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis".
  • 1954-Died due to cyanide poisoning, possibly from an apple which was found half-eaten at his bedside. Turing was known to reguraly eat an apple before going to bed. Several other theories exist about the cause of his cyanide poisoning.
  • 1966-The Turing award is inaugurated.
